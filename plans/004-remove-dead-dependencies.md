## Why this matters

`@jpmorgan-payments/embedded-finance-components` is a **published npm package**; its `dependencies` are installed by every consumer. `yup` (^1.7.1) sits in runtime `dependencies` with **zero imports anywhere in `src/`** — the library standardized on zod (62+ import sites; `package.json`'s own `catalog.validation` field says `"zod"`). Listing yup also falsely signals to integrators that yup schemas are supported. Separately, two devDependencies are leftovers from the old Mantine stack: `postcss-preset-mantine` and `postcss-simple-vars` are loaded by nothing — `postcss.config.js` uses only `tailwindcss` + `autoprefixer`. Removing all three shrinks installs and removes misleading signals at near-zero risk.
